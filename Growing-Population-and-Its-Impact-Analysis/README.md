**This project performs data cleaning, transformation, and exploratory data analysis (EDA) on population growth data to identify trends, growth patterns, and their impact on economic and social factors.**

This study presents a comprehensive analysis of India's key development indicators from 2000 to 2023 using an interactive data visualization dashboard. The analysis focuses on major socio-economic and environmental factors including population growth, GDP per capita, unemployment rate, urbanization, CO₂ emissions, and water consumption. By integrating multiple datasets and visual analytics, the dashboard enables users to explore trends, patterns, and relationships among these indicators across different states in India.

The economic analysis highlights a steady increase in GDP per capita over time, accompanied by fluctuations in unemployment rates. Environmental insights reveal a gradual rise in CO₂ emissions and water consumption, which appear to correlate with increasing population and economic activity. Population analysis shows consistent population growth with variations in urban and rural population distribution. Additionally, urbanization trends indicate that states with higher economic performance tend to exhibit higher urban population percentages.

The interactive visualizations, including maps, trend lines, bar charts, and scatter plots, provide an intuitive way to understand regional disparities and long-term development trends. Overall, the study demonstrates how data visualization can support data-driven insights into India's economic development, environmental sustainability, and demographic changes.
